## Architecture note: anchor-based alignment

The `run_multi_strategy_backtest.py` was updated to use anchor-based alignment.
Only symbols with data back to `args.start` define the common trading-date grid.
Mid-window IPOs (META 2012, TSLA 2010, V 2008) are merged onto this grid with
empty bars in early years — `signals.py` naturally excludes them from top-N
selection until they accumulate `momentum_window_days` (126) of history.
This fixes the prior issue where `align_bars()` would snap the entire window
to 2013 because META's first bar is 2012-05-18.

## Crisis-period focus (2008-06 → 2009-10)

The most striking result: **the deepest crisis-window drawdown was only
~8% — the trend filters did the heavy lifting and the circuit-breaker
softened the rest.**

| Date | Event | Portfolio | DD from peak |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| 2008-06-02 | Backtest start (effective signals begin) | $100,000 | 0.00% |
| 2008-09-15 | Lehman Brothers collapse | — | — |
| 2008-09-25 | **First FULL→HALF transition** (10 days post-Lehman) | $97,810 | 8.18% |
| 2009-03-09 | SPY crisis low (SPY -55% from peak; B&H DD 50.70%) | (still HALF) | < 8.5% |
| 2009-10-07 | **HALF→FULL recovery** | $101,342 | 4.86% |

**Recovery time from crisis trough to back-to-FULL: ~12 months** (2008-09-25
→ 2009-10-07).

### Why the crisis DD was only ~8%

By mid-2008 SPY had broken below its 10-month moving average. That trend-filter
break drives two of the three strategies to cash:

- **Strategy A (TAA, 60%)** — explicit 10-month SMA filter. When SPY < 10-mo
  SMA it rotates to the cash proxy (BIL/SHV).
- **Strategy B (large-cap momentum, 30%)** — requires SPY > 10-month SMA to
  hold any positions. When SPY breaks below, B is forced to cash.
- **Strategy C (gold overlay, 10%)** — permanent GLD. Gold *rose* during the
  2008-09 crisis flight-to-safety, partially offsetting equity losses.

So at the moment of the crash, the portfolio was effectively ~90% cash + 10%
gold. The 8% peak-to-trough is mostly trend-filter signal lag (the SMA break
doesn't fire until after some decline has happened) plus equity volatility
on the days before the breaker engaged.

### The worst DD wasn't in 2008 — it was 2017

The 12.67% max drawdown for the full window came from **2017-08-09**
(FULL→HALF at 12.67%), with a HALF→OUT transition two days later
(2017-08-11 at 12.03%). That was a brief sharp move where the trend filters
didn't have time to fully engage. The OUT state then protected the portfolio
from further loss; OUT→HALF recovery took until 2020-03-13 (the COVID crash
dropped the recovery threshold). 2017 is the more useful stress test of the
breaker's responsiveness than 2008 itself.

## Per-strategy attribution (standalone returns, not portfolio share)

The underlying backtest reports what each strategy would have grown its
allocated capital into if run independently — not its contribution to
final portfolio equity. The portfolio's actual blended return is the
11.95% / yr headline; the standalone numbers below are a diagnostic.

| Strategy | Allocation | Standalone return | Standalone final | Trades |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| dual_momentum_taa | 60% | +335.95% | $261,568 | 231 |
| large_cap_momentum_top5 | 30% | **+5,351.08%** | $1,635,323 | 513 |
| gold_permanent_overlay | 10% | +390.65% | $49,065 | 1 |

The +5,351% on Strategy B is **the smoking gun for survivor bias.** A
2026-curated basket of 20 large-caps, run from 2008 onward, includes only
companies that survived to be in that basket — Lehman, AIG, Citigroup,
Wachovia, Countrywide, Bear Stearns, and dozens of other 2008-era large
caps that *didn't* survive are absent. The realistic 2008-onward Strategy
B return is dramatically lower than +5,351%; how much lower is unknown
until the survivor-bias-corrected backtest lands (see plan item
`2026-05-12-survivor-bias-stress-test.md`). For now, treat any forward
return forecast on Strategy B with extreme caution — this is the largest
single haircut hanging over the production strategy mix.

## Caveats and known biases

- **yfinance survivor bias on Strategy B is amplified in this window.** 
  Even with the time-aware listing filter and anchor-based alignment, the
  20-name basket is the *2026 winners* set. Names that *would have been in a
  2008-era large-cap basket but later collapsed* (e.g. Citigroup, AIG, Lehman)
  are absent. See `2026-05-12-survivor-bias-stress-test.md` plan for the fix.
- **Cash proxy substitution:** BIL has slightly different yield characteristics 
  than SHV; expect a few bps difference in Strategy A cash-floor returns.
- **First real signals:** ~12 months after the start date (252-day momentum warmup),
  so effective trading window is approximately 2008-06 onward.

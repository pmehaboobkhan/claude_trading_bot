## Alignment constraint — why 2008 was not reached

The multi-strategy backtest uses `align_bars()` which intersects trading dates across
ALL symbols, including Strategy B's universe. Even with `filter_bars_by_listing()`
correctly dropping pre-listing bars for META/TSLA/V, META's first bar is 2012-05-18.
Combined with the 12-month momentum warmup window, the earliest date where Strategy B
can fire is 2013-05-22. `align_bars` then snaps ALL strategies (A, B, C) to this
common start.

**Consequence:** The 2008 financial crisis (SPY peak Oct 2007 → trough Mar 2009) is
entirely outside the effective backtest window. The MaxDD of 12.68% comes from the
2024 drawdown (Jul–Aug 2024), not from 2008.

**To genuinely stress-test through 2008**, the architecture would need to run Strategy A
(TAA: SPY/IEF/GLD/BIL, all available pre-2008) independently on its own aligned window,
and report crisis-period drawdown for that sub-strategy separately. This is a future
work item (see plan.md "Still open").

## Caveats and known biases

- **2008 crisis NOT tested.** The effective window starts 2013-05-22 due to META's
  listing date and the align_bars intersection requirement. This is the primary caveat.
- **yfinance survivor bias on Strategy B is amplified.** Even with the time-aware
  listing filter, the 20-name basket is the *2026 winners* set. Names that collapsed
  (Citigroup, AIG, Lehman) are absent.
- **Cash proxy substitution:** BIL has slightly different yield characteristics than SHV.
  Observed CAGR difference is ~50 bps over the 12.9y window.
- **COVID as proxy stress:** The 2020 crash provides some crisis evidence. Portfolio DD
  of 12.03% against a 34% SPY crash implies a 64% drawdown reduction vs buy-and-hold.
